    Origami has evolved over time and began to focus on mobile payment service to improve shopping experience and connect consumers and stores. In order to achieve this we needed to evolve our product and simplify the consumer experience to support methods of payment while educating customers about the discounts at available stores.

    Objective:

      In the initial phase we needed to consider many conditions as we did not have such a great store volumes to offer, however these are the significant points that we took into account in order to create a best experience for our users with our store volumes at that time:


    • Focus on showing the best content rather than showing everything.
    • Primarily we should have the control of our curated content.
    • Manage content accordingly to your area and interest. Static content does not work for everyone.
    • Make sure to offer many type of content so that we can learn which content is more useful over time. We can use this knowledge to then build a better shop discovery feature (once we have greater shop volume) as we will know which stores resinate with people the most.

    Wireframe & UX

    Exploration

    Early web experiment that didn't work

    It turns out that we couldn't provide best content with this design as it can only show one type of category one at the time and takes effort to switch between categories. One of the merit for the verticle list is that you can show many stores in one view, however with the volume of stores we had at that time it was not the UI we wanted.

    Quick web prototype that we made with using actual store information API.

    UI & UX brainstorming

    Learning from prior experiment with a quick web prototype we decided to design a UI that is able to show as many types of categories as possible. This way we can curate and focus on the high quality content in a single page. Our intension was to make this feature as effortless as possible while showing the most valuable content for customers.

    Some of the high fidelity exploration we made through many critiques.

    Outcome

    We decided it made most sense to make each sections corousel to have various categories in a single page so it shows few most interesting content from each of them. As people have different preferences and interests, we also used cobination of geo-location and data from users such as Viewed stores, whether they are Male/Female and Purchased history to tailor content for each users.
